# SOP & TRAINING ENGINE — PROMPT FRAMEWORK
## For Use with ChatGPT, Claude, Kimi, or Any Large Language Model

---

### WHAT THIS IS

This document contains a master prompt template and ten scenario-specific prompt modules. Copy the master prompt into any AI tool, then append the relevant scenario module. The AI will generate a complete SOP, compliance check, training script, and quiz for that exact situation.

This framework is designed for Store Managers and Lead Associates who need to create training materials quickly without writing from scratch.

---

### MASTER PROMPT TEMPLATE

Copy and paste the following into your AI tool. Replace the bracketed sections with your specific details.

```
You are a senior compliance officer and retail training specialist for The Forest, a cannabis dispensary in Cleveland Heights, Ohio. The Forest is a retail brand of Standard Wellness Holdings, LLC, operating under Ohio Division of Cannabis Control (DCC) regulations.

Your task is to generate a complete training package for the following scenario:

SCENARIO: [Describe the situation in one sentence.]

AUDIENCE: Dispensary floor staff, including new hires and experienced budtenders.

TONE: Professional, clear, empathetic, and legally precise. Never casual. Never judgmental.

OUTPUT FORMAT: Generate exactly four sections:

SECTION 1 — STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)
Provide a numbered, step-by-step procedure. Each step must be actionable in thirty seconds or less. Include the exact words staff should say to the customer where applicable. Include escalation triggers (when to call a manager).

SECTION 2 — OHIO DCC COMPLIANCE CHECK
List every Ohio law, regulation, or recordkeeping requirement relevant to this scenario. Cite specific consequences of non-compliance (license suspension, felony charges, fines). Include documentation requirements.

SECTION 3 — TWO-MINUTE TRAINING SCRIPT
Write a script that a manager can read aloud to staff during a pre-shift huddle. Include one role-play scenario. Include one common mistake to avoid. End with a motivational closing line.

SECTION 4 — THREE QUIZ QUESTIONS
Write three multiple-choice questions to verify staff understanding. Each question must have one correct answer and three plausible distractors. Provide the correct answer and a brief explanation for each.

CONSTRAINTS:
- Do not recommend any action that violates Ohio DCC regulations.
- Do not suggest giving unofficial discounts or free products without manager approval.
- Do not assume the customer is lying. Assume good faith while protecting the business.
- All customer-facing language must be respectful and non-confrontational.
- If the scenario involves a medical patient, prioritize patient safety and continuity of care.
```

### SCENARIO MODULES

Append one of the following modules to the master prompt. Each module adds specific context and constraints for that scenario.

---

#### MODULE 1: CUSTOMER DISPUTES OVERCHARGE

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- The Forest has experienced pricing discrepancies between its online menu and in-store POS system.
- Some registers have a "forced best discount" logic that applies incorrect discounts.
- One employee has been documented rounding up customer totals to the nearest dollar.
- Ohio DCC requires all refunds to be documented in METRC-adjacent records.
- The Forest policy allows staff to offer a $5 loyalty credit for verified overcharges without manager approval.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include a step for reviewing the receipt WITH the customer, not alone behind the counter.
- Include a step for checking the online menu price in real-time on a secondary device.
- Address the rounding-up practice explicitly: it must stop immediately.
- Include the exact phrase staff should use to apologize without admitting fault.
```

---

#### MODULE 2: ID VERIFICATION PROBLEM

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- Ohio law requires valid government-issued photo ID for all adult-use purchases.
- Medical patients must present an active Ohio Medical Marijuana Registry card.
- Out-of-state IDs are accepted for adult-use but not for medical registry verification.
- Sale to a minor is a felony under Ohio Revised Code 3796.22.
- Staff have reported customers becoming hostile when ID is refused.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the four-point ID check: government-issued, not expired, photo matches, age verified.
- Include the exact phrase for refusing sale due to expired ID.
- Include de-escalation language for hostile customers.
- Include documentation requirements for refused sales.
```

---

#### MODULE 3: DRIVE-THRU LINE BACKUP

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- The Forest Cleveland Heights has a drive-thru window.
- Drive-thru is a competitive advantage but becomes a bottleneck during peak hours.
- The POS system has experienced outages during high-volume periods.
- Staff have reported pressure to skip ID verification in the drive-thru to speed up the line.
- Ohio DCC requires the same compliance standards for drive-thru as in-store.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include car-count thresholds for calling backup (5 cars, 10 cars).
- Include a manual order verification process for POS outages.
- Explicitly state that ID verification cannot be skipped regardless of line length.
- Include a customer communication script for wait times.
```

---

#### MODULE 4: MEDICAL VS. ADULT-USE CONFUSION

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- Ohio operates a dual medical and adult-use cannabis system.
- Medical and adult-use transactions must be tracked separately in METRC.
- Medical patients receive a 25% discount (veteran, indigent, or standard medical group rate).
- Adult-use customers do not receive medical discounts.
- Tax rates differ between medical and adult-use purchases.
- Some customers are confused about which category they fall into.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the exact question staff must ask every customer: "Are you shopping as a medical patient today, or adult-use?"
- Explain the two-transaction rule for customers who want both.
- Include the tax difference explanation.
- Emphasize that staff must never make a customer feel stupid for not understanding the dual system.
```

---

#### MODULE 5: PRODUCT RETURN REQUEST

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- Ohio DCC prohibits the return of opened or partially consumed cannabis products.
- Defective products (broken seal, mold, incorrect labeling) may be returned with documentation.
- Some customers are unsatisfied with product effects and want refunds.
- The Forest policy allows replacement or refund for defective products.
- All returns must be documented with METRC batch numbers.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the exact phrase for refusing an opened-product return while maintaining empathy.
- Include the escalation trigger for defective products exceeding $100.
- Include the quarantine procedure for defective products.
- Address the "effects" complaint: cannabis affects individuals differently, and this is not grounds for return.
```

---

#### MODULE 6: CUSTOMER WANTS OUT-OF-STOCK ITEM

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- The Forest has experienced stockouts of popular strains, particularly The Standard GG4 and Galenas Eleventh Dimension.
- Medical patients may be dependent on specific strains for symptom management.
- The Forest does not currently have a formal restock alert system.
- Competitor Amplify Dispensary is located one mile away on Coventry Road.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the exact apology phrase that acknowledges the inconvenience without making excuses.
- Include the alternative recommendation process using staff product knowledge.
- Include the medical patient escalation trigger.
- Include the restock alert capture process (name + phone + preferred product).
```

---

#### MODULE 7: HOSTILE OR RUDE CUSTOMER

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- Staff have reported verbal abuse, intoxicated customers, and occasional threats.
- One review mentioned an employee being "called a liar" and another being subjected to racially charged arguments.
- The Forest is located in a progressive suburb but serves a diverse regional customer base.
- Staff safety is the highest priority.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the de-escalation mantra: "Stay calm. Do not match their energy. Lower your voice."
- Include the exact boundary-setting phrase for profanity or personal attacks.
- Include the 911 trigger: physical threats or aggressive behavior.
- Include the team debrief requirement after any hostile incident.
```

---

#### MODULE 8: BULK PURCHASE / DISCOUNT REQUEST

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- Ohio DCC prohibits quantity-based pricing that incentivizes purchases above legal limits.
- The Forest offers a 25% daily group discount for veterans, medical patients, and indigent customers with documentation.
- Some regular customers request unofficial "bulk rates" or "hookups."
- One employee review mentioned that management "only cares about money," creating pressure to bend rules.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the exact phrase for refusing an unofficial discount while preserving the customer relationship.
- Include the three groups that qualify for the 25% discount and the documentation required for each.
- Explain why quantity discounts are prohibited in Ohio.
- Include the POS documentation requirement for every discount applied.
```

---

#### MODULE 9: VISIBLY INTOXICATED CUSTOMER

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- Ohio Revised Code 3796.22 prohibits sale to intoxicated persons.
- Staff have reported customers arriving already impaired by alcohol or other substances.
- Some intoxicated customers arrive with sober companions who attempt to purchase on their behalf (proxy purchase).
- Staff fear retaliation or lost tips if they refuse service.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the observation-first approach: assess from a distance before confronting.
- Include the exact refusal phrase that is firm but non-judgmental.
- Include the proxy purchase rule: sober companions cannot buy for an intoxicated person.
- Include the staff protection statement: "You cannot be punished for refusing an illegal sale."
```

---

#### MODULE 10: THEFT OR SHRINKAGE SUSPECTED

```
ADDITIONAL CONTEXT:
- The Forest has experienced register shorts and suspected product theft.
- Staff are not trained security personnel and should not confront suspected thieves directly.
- Security footage must be retained for 30 days minimum per Ohio DCC requirements.
- One employee review mentioned "call the cops on you if you don't do what they say," indicating a culture of fear around management.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS:
- Include the observation-and-document rule: never confront directly.
- Include the discreet notification protocol (text, whisper, code word).
- Include the METRC inventory reconciliation step before assuming theft.
- Include the documentation requirements for all suspected incidents.
```

### HOW TO USE THIS FRAMEWORK

**For a pre-built scenario:**
1. Open the SOP Training Engine HTML file.
2. Click the scenario button.
3. The tool generates the complete package instantly.

**For a custom scenario:**
1. Copy the Master Prompt Template.
2. Describe your scenario in the SCENARIO field.
3. Add any relevant context from the modules above.
4. Paste into ChatGPT, Claude, Kimi, or any AI tool.
5. Review the output for accuracy. AI can make mistakes. Always verify against Ohio DCC regulations.
6. Save the output. Add it to your store's SOP library.

**For recurring training:**
1. Pick one scenario per week.
2. Generate the training package five minutes before the pre-shift huddle.
3. Read the Two-Minute Training Script aloud.
4. Conduct the role-play.
5. Ask the three quiz questions.
6. Document attendance and scores.

### QUALITY ASSURANCE CHECKLIST

Before using any AI-generated SOP, verify:
- [ ] Every step is actionable in 30 seconds or less.
- [ ] Customer-facing language is respectful and non-confrontational.
- [ ] No step violates Ohio DCC regulations.
- [ ] Escalation triggers are specific (dollar amounts, behavior descriptions).
- [ ] Documentation requirements are explicit.
- [ ] The training script includes a role-play scenario.
- [ ] Quiz questions have one unambiguous correct answer.
- [ ] The output has been reviewed by the Store Manager or Lead Associate.
